Lesson 3

Cinematic Video Editing with Canva

Welcome

Welcome to Lesson 3. In Lessons 1 and 2, you learned how to edit videos using Canva. Today, you will learn how to make your videos look more professional by creating smooth, engaging, and cinematic videos that capture your audience's attention. By the end of this lesson, you should be able to produce a short video that looks polished and tells a clear story.

Lesson Objectives

After completing this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Create a cinematic opening scene.
  • Plan a simple story before editing.
  • Use B-roll footage to improve your videos.
  • Match video clips with background music.
  • Create smooth zoom and pan effects.
  • Add subtitles and captions.
  • Balance music and voice clearly.
  • Export videos for different social media platforms.
STEP 1

Plan Your Video Before Editing

Professional editors do not start by dragging random clips into the timeline. They first decide what story they want to tell.

  1. Choose the topic of your video.
  2. Write down the beginning.
  3. Write the middle part.
  4. Write the ending.
  5. Gather all the videos and pictures you will need.
Practice Activity Plan a one-minute video about: "My School" Write: • Beginning • Middle • Ending before opening Canva.
STEP 2

Create a Strong Opening Scene

The first few seconds determine whether viewers will continue watching your video.

  1. Create a new page.
  2. Choose a beautiful background.
  3. Add a bold title.
  4. Add your name or logo.
  5. Animate the title.
  6. Keep the opening between 3 and 5 seconds.
A simple opening with clean text often looks more professional than one with too many animations.
STEP 3

Use B-Roll Footage

B-roll is extra video footage that supports your main story. It makes your videos more interesting and keeps viewers engaged.

Examples of B-Roll

  • Students walking into class.
  • Books on a desk.
  • Typing on a computer.
  • People smiling.
  • Nature or city views.
  • Close-up shots of objects.
  1. Add your main video.
  2. Create another page.
  3. Add a short B-roll clip.
  4. Continue alternating between the main video and B-roll clips.
Create a project using: • One introduction clip • Two B-roll clips • One closing clip Notice how the B-roll helps tell the story.
STEP 4

Match Video Clips with Music

Music should support your video instead of distracting from it.

  1. Add a music track.
  2. Listen to the beat carefully.
  3. Trim your clips so they change naturally with the rhythm of the music.
  4. Preview the video several times.
Good timing makes even simple videos feel more exciting and professional.
STEP 5

Create Zoom and Pan Effects

One of the easiest ways to make your videos look cinematic is by adding movement to still images and video clips. Slow movement keeps your audience interested.

  1. Add a picture to your project.
  2. Resize the picture so it fills the screen.
  3. Select Animate if available.
  4. Try animations such as Pan, Rise, or Drift.
  5. If using a video, crop it carefully to focus attention on the subject.
  6. Preview the movement and adjust it until it looks smooth.
Practice: Create one page using a landscape photo and apply a slow movement effect to make it look like the camera is moving.
STEP 6

Create Professional Title Screens

Every professional video should have a clean title that introduces the topic.

  1. Create a new page.
  2. Choose a simple background.
  3. Add a bold title.
  4. Add a smaller subtitle below it.
  5. Use only one or two font styles.
  6. Animate the title with a simple effect.
Avoid using too many colours or fonts. Clean and simple titles are easier to read.

STEP 7

Add Subtitles and Captions

Subtitles help viewers understand your message, especially when they are watching without sound.

  1. Play your video.
  2. Pause where someone starts speaking.
  3. Add a text box.
  4. Type exactly what is being said.
  5. Position the subtitle near the bottom of the screen.
  6. Repeat this process throughout the video.
Use a large, easy-to-read font with good colour contrast so viewers can read the subtitles clearly.
STEP 8

Balance Music and Voice

Background music should support your narration, not overpower it.

  1. Select the music track.
  2. Reduce the music volume.
  3. Play the video with your voice recording.
  4. Adjust the music until your voice is clear.
  5. Preview the entire project.
Practice: Record a short voice introduction and adjust the music until every word can be heard clearly.
STEP 9

Create Smooth Scene Changes

Professional editors choose transitions carefully. Too many flashy transitions can distract viewers.

  1. Open the transition menu.
  2. Choose one transition style.
  3. Apply the same transition throughout the project.
  4. Adjust the transition speed.
  5. Preview the result.

Recommended Transitions

  • Fade
  • Dissolve
  • Slide
  • Match & Move
Using one or two transition styles consistently usually looks more professional than using a different transition for every scene.
STEP 10

Export Videos for Different Platforms

Different social media platforms work better with different video sizes and lengths. Before downloading your video, think about where you want to publish it.

Platform Recommended Format
YouTube Landscape (16:9)
TikTok Vertical (9:16)
Instagram Reels Vertical (9:16)
Facebook Reels Vertical (9:16)
WhatsApp Status Vertical (9:16)

  1. Click Share.
  2. Select Download.
  3. Choose MP4 Video.
  4. Select the highest quality available.
  5. Download your project.
Always watch your exported video before posting it online.
STEP 11

Review Your Finished Video

Professional editors rarely publish the first version of a video. They watch it carefully and improve it before sharing.

Review Checklist

  • ✔ Is the title easy to read?
  • ✔ Are all clips in the correct order?
  • ✔ Does the music fit the mood?
  • ✔ Is the narration clear?
  • ✔ Are subtitles correctly placed?
  • ✔ Are transitions smooth?
  • ✔ Is the ending satisfying?
Ask a friend or classmate to watch your video and give feedback before publishing it.
