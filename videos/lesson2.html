Lesson 2

Advanced Canva Video Editing

Welcome Back!

Congratulations on completing Lesson 1. In this lesson, you will begin editing videos like a professional by combining multiple videos, adding advanced effects, and making your projects more attractive. Everything in this lesson is practical. Follow each step with your teacher.

Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Combine several videos into one project.
  • Split videos accurately.
  • Trim unwanted parts.
  • Arrange clips in the correct order.
  • Create smooth storytelling using multiple scenes.
  • Build a professional-looking video.
STEP 1

Open an Existing Project

  1. Open Canva.
  2. Go to Projects.
  3. Select the project you created in Lesson 1.
  4. If you don't have one, create a new Mobile Video.
Professional editors rarely start from scratch every time. Most projects are edited several times before they are completed.
STEP 2

Add Multiple Video Clips

Instead of using only one video, let's use several clips.

  1. Click Uploads.
  2. Select three different short videos.
  3. Upload all of them.
  4. Drag the first video to Page One.
  5. Create another page.
  6. Add the second video.
  7. Create another page.
  8. Add the third video.
Practice: Arrange your videos like a short story. Example: Scene 1 → Introduction Scene 2 → Main Activity Scene 3 → Ending
STEP 3

Split and Trim Videos

Sometimes you only need part of a video.

  1. Select the video.
  2. Move the playhead to where you want to cut.
  3. Click Split.
  4. Delete the unwanted section.
  5. Trim the remaining clip.
Practice: Take a 20-second video. Split it into two parts. Delete one part and keep only the best section.
STEP 4

Arrange Clips on the Timeline

The timeline controls the order in which your audience watches the video.

  1. Press and hold a video clip.
  2. Drag it left or right.
  3. Rearrange the order.
  4. Preview your changes.
Good storytelling depends on arranging scenes in the correct order.
STEP 5

Work with Layers

Layers allow you to place one object on top of another. This is useful for placing text, pictures, stickers, or logos over a video.

  1. Add a video to your page.
  2. Click Text and add a heading.
  3. Move the text to the top of the video.
  4. Add a picture or logo.
  5. Resize it and place it in one corner.
  6. If an object is hidden, use Position to move it Forward or Backward.
Practice: Create a page with:
  • One full-screen video
  • Your name at the top
  • A small logo or picture in the bottom-right corner
STEP 6

Create a Picture-in-Picture (PIP) Video

Picture-in-Picture allows you to show one video while another small video appears on top.

  1. Add your main video.
  2. Add another video to the same page.
  3. Resize the second video.
  4. Move it to a corner.
  5. Add a border or shadow if available.
This effect is commonly used in tutorials, reaction videos, interviews, and online classes.
STEP 7

Apply Filters

Filters can completely change the mood of your video.

  1. Select your video.
  2. Click Edit.
  3. Choose Filters.
  4. Try different filters.
  5. Select the one you like best.

Try These Filters

  • Warm
  • Cool
  • Retro
  • Mono
  • Vivid
Preview each filter before deciding which one improves your video.
STEP 8

Adjust Brightness and Colour

Instead of using filters, you can manually improve your video.

  1. Select the video.
  2. Click Adjust.
  3. Increase or decrease Brightness.
  4. Adjust Contrast.
  5. Adjust Saturation.
  6. Adjust Warmth.
  7. Preview the result.
Practice: Take a dark video and make it brighter without making it look unnatural.
STEP 9

Use Canva Frames

Frames allow you to place pictures or videos inside creative shapes.

  1. Click Elements.
  2. Search for Frames.
  3. Choose a circle, square, phone, or computer frame.
  4. Drag a picture or video into the frame.
  5. Double-click to reposition it.
Create one page with:
  • A full-screen background video
  • A circular frame showing your picture
  • Your name underneath the frame
STEP 10

Control Video Speed

Changing the playback speed can make your video more exciting.

  1. Select the video.
  2. Click Playback Speed (if available).
  3. Slow the video down.
  4. Play it.
  5. Increase the speed.
  6. Compare both results.
Slow motion is great for dramatic moments, while faster speeds work well for time-lapse videos and quick demonstrations.
STEP 11

Synchronize Music with Your Video

A good video editor makes sure that the music matches what is happening in the video.

  1. Click on the audio track.
  2. Drag the music left or right to change where it starts.
  3. Trim the beginning or end if necessary.
  4. Reduce the volume when someone is speaking.
  5. Play the project several times to check if the music fits each scene.
Practice: Use one music track for your entire project and make sure it starts smoothly with the first scene and ends at the last scene.
STEP 12

Create an Intro and Outro

Every professional video should have a beginning (Intro) and an ending (Outro).

Create the Intro

  1. Add a new page at the beginning.
  2. Add a background colour or picture.
  3. Add your video title.
  4. Add your name.
  5. Animate the text.
  6. Keep the intro between 3 and 5 seconds.

Create the Outro

  1. Add a new page at the end.
  2. Write Thank You for Watching.
  3. Add your name or class name.
  4. Add a simple animation.
  5. Keep the outro between 3 and 5 seconds.
An attractive intro and outro make your video look complete and professional.
STEP 13

Export Your Video

  1. Click Share.
  2. Select Download.
  3. Choose MP4 Video.
  4. Select the highest quality available.
  5. Click Download.
  6. Watch the exported video to make sure everything is correct.
Always preview your exported video before sharing it with others.
